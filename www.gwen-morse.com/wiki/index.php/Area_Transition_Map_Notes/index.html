Area Transitions

  • Link back to The Long Dark Mapping
  • Link back to Tips for The Long Dark Noobs

Here are the assorted area transitions I found, with varying levels of guidance.

  • Coastal Highway (From Mystery Lake): Follow the Southern River Access to Carter Hydroelectric Dam. If you walk up by the door but stay outside and then turn right at the single tall tree and head into a channel between rocks it leads to a transition point called "Ravine". To travel through the Ravine ...
  1. Travel the pass until you find a small bridge. Cross the bridge.
  2. Find the train derailment. Walk around the train on the right side. It will look like you're going to walk off the edge if the cliff. VERY CAREFULLY work your way around the path until you find a dead body with a backpack.
  3. At the dead body, walk across the fallen tree to the train car. Step off onto the car. Follow the path away from the car.
  4. Cross the shiny green bridge.
  5. You'll end up on a broken train rail. Follow the rail to the train tunnel with clutter in it. Go inside the tunnel and around the clutter. If you're going the right way you can pass through the back of the tunnel.
  6. On the far side of the tunnel is a very high and rickety-looking train bridge. Very carefully make your way across the bridge. The rails on one side are broken. It's recommended you crouch when crossing the broken rails.
  7. There's another tunnel on the far side of the rickety train pass that leads to the Coastal Highway zone.
  • Mystery Lake (from Coastal Highway). From the :og Sort, cross the paved road and take the road up into the mountains that veers off to the left. Follow this winding road until you hit the train tracks, follow the tracks to the left (the side that doesn't have the train cars of logs) until you walk into a tunnel and are transferred to the Ravine. Then reverse the steps above.
  • Pleasant Valley (From Coastal Highway): Get to the Abandoned Lookout (it's above and behind the Quonset Garage). There's a logging road that leads to it near the Coastal Townsite. Once you reach the Lookout, keep going on the road. If you lose track of directions, stand with your back to the Abandoned Lookout and the path in front of you, then turn left. You'll eventually find an Abandoned Mine that's chock *full* of coal. You'll want a torch or lantern or flare for light. To travel through the Abandoned Mine ...
  1. Enter the mine and keep going straight. The mine is a big maze. I found my route through it by always turning left.
  2. Keep moving deeper into the mine, looting and picking up coal.
  3. You know you're near the end of your route when you find a rickety-looking wooden scaffold being used as stairs. Climb the stairs.
  4. Exit through the end of the tunnel.
  • Coastal Highway (From Pleasant Valley). Follow the main road/power lines out of town. You'll be heading in a generally north-ish direction, both the Pleasant Valley Homestead and the Barn should be on the left side of the road. Just after the burned out Abandoned Settlement will be a path on the right with a fallen tree you can run under. Turn onto this path. On the right side you'll see a broad, snow covered trail, follow this until you reach the mine.
  • Timberwolf Mountain (from Pleasant Valley). Find the picnic area. Climb the reddish/brownish rock climbing rope handing down from the big cliff. Make sure you've rested (to regain all stamina) and eaten recently.
