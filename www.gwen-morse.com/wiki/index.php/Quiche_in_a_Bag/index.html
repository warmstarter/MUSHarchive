Quiche in a Bag

Note from Gwen: Quiche in a bag is a pretty common recipe on the internet. I got mine from: http://simply-saving.com/easy-once-a-month-recipes/ but there’s pretty much no variation in the versions other than the leftovers you toss in.

I will note this is not a real quiche. The quiche-like structure comes from the eggs and flour. It's just a nice way to re-use leftovers so they don't get thrown out.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cooked sausage or diced ham or diced roast beef
  • 10 oz. frozen package chopped spinach or broccoli, thawed a little and broken up.
  • 1 cup cheddar cheese
  • 1 small onion chopped
  • 2 cups milk
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon Tobasco or Piri Piri sauce
  • 1/2 cup white or whole wheat flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder

Directions

Mix eggs, milk, flour and baking powder with a wisk or mixer or blender until well incorporated. Add meat, veggies, cheese and onion. Pour into a freezer bag. Freeze. This works best if you freeze it flat on a cookie sheet first and then stack after it's solid. You can vary the meat and veggies each time you make it.

To cook, thaw bag, squish the bag well a few times to incorporate the ingredients. Pour into a pie plate sprayed with non stick spray or rubbed with olive oil. Sprinkle with paprika or red pepper flakes if desired.

Bake at 350F (180 C) for 35-45 minutes until set.

Variations

1) Spinach on the side - I don't like to partially thaw veggies and then refreeze them. I just defrost a box of spinach when I defrost the quiche, and then toss the contents into the bag when "squishing". If I'm adding broccoli I use fresh and I cut it into small florets.

2) Boiling Bag - Take a bag out of the freezer and toss it into a pot of boiling water for about 10-15 minutes (watch it to make sure it doesn't melt). Remove, dump out the bag (carefully of course, since it's hot). Salt and pepper to taste and enjoy. This is the “Oh damn, forgot to defrost something” solution.

3) Crust - Defrost, and dump into a baked Bachelor Pie Crust base. Add a sprinkle of paprika to the crust before mixing. Bake at 350F (180C) for 35-45 minutes until set.
