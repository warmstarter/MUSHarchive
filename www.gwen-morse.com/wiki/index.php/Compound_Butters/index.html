Compound Butters

EVERYTHING IS BETTER WITH BUTTER! There are a few cases where that isn't true. However, butter is one of those basic cooking ingredients that's so tasty and versatile that everyone seems to love it..

I love to make compound butters, which are a mixture of butter and herbs and spices. The pat of butter melted over a fancy steak or expensive filet of fish is often a compound butter. There are also sweet compound butters. One of the most famous is the paste made of butter, cinnamon, and brown sugar that is used for cinnamon rolls.

The basic technique to making a compound butter is to soften a stick or block of nice quality butter to room temperature. Blend in a mix of herbs and spices using either firm hand mixing or an electric mixer. Turn the resulting blob of flavored butter onto a piece of wax paper or plastic wrap. Roll it up into a tidy little "log" shape and twist the ends (so it looks like a giant Tootsie Roll or piece of taffy. Let it sit at room temperature for about an hour (to let the flavors combine). It's very important to let the butter rest at room temperature for that hour otherwise the flavor will be "off". Then refrigerate. It can be frozen if the butter hasn't been previously frozen.

If you store multiple packs of compound butter, it's probably a good idea to label them. I've been in the unfortunate position of asking my son "Does it look like it has lemon zest in it? Or do those little flecks look more like chopped peanuts?" as we both peer at a frozen butter log.

Note: Even though all the recipes call for unsalted butter, I use a nice quality of _salted_ butter. I very rarely add salt in my cooking (other than salted butter or salt-packed herbs) and I just prefer the slightly extra saltiness. Even sweet compound butters benefit from a little salt. Don't be afraid to give it a try, you just might like it. Of course, if you use a salted butter, don't add extra salt in the recipe.

Savory Compound Butters

Easy Basil Compound Butter

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 clove garlic, finely minced
  • 1/4 cup basil leaves, finely minced
  • sea salt, to taste

Garlic Cilantro Butter

  • 1/4 cup butter, softened
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 Tablespoon chopped cilantro leaves
  • 1 teaspoon lime zest
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Basil Parmesan Compound Butter

  • 8 Tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped basil
  • 1 small clove garlic finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon fine sea salt

Kalamata Olive Butter

  • 8 Tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 small clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon finely chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup pitted chopped kalamata olives

Lemon Dill Butter

  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh dill
  • the zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice

Sweet Compound Butters

Chocolate Hazelnut Butter

  • 8 Tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • Powdered cocoa to taste
  • Sugar to taste
  • 1 to 2 tablespoons finely chopped hazelnuts

Creamy Brown Sugar Butter

  • 8 Tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar

Honey Butter

  • 8 Tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup honey (that's 8 Tablespoons since honey can be hard to scrape out of a cup).

Honey Fruit Butter

  • 8 Tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons fresh-squeezed orange juice
  • 1 small banana

Marmalade Butter

  • 8 Tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup marmalade (lemon, orange, or grapefruit)

Raisin-Nut Butter

  • 8 Tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped toasted walnuts
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped raisins
  • 1 tablespoon fresh-squeezed orange juice
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Rum Raisin Butter

  • 8 Tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 tablespoon raisins or currants
  • 2 tablespoons rum
  • Sugar to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Sweet Citrus Butter

  • 8 Tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 teaspoons grated orange or lemon zest
  • 1 tablespoon fresh-squeezed orange or lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon

White Chocolate Butter

  • 8 Tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons white chocolate, melted and cooled
  • Ground cinnamon to taste


Making Butter Spreadable From the Fridge Mix one cup of butter with 3/4 cup of minimally-processed oil (light unadulterated olive oil or grapeseed oil). If you can trust the source of your "light" olive oil to be _pure_ olive oil, or you can get grapeseed oil, your butter will still taste like butter but be spreadable out of the fridge, with the added bonus of all the health benefits from the oil.
