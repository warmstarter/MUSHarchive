In the WoD, Attributes can be increased by spending experience points. The same is true in the base Omni system (however it's NOT true in Talislanta). Use the base Omni system if you want to spend XP on attributes.

Abilities are broken up into discrete categories in the WoD, and include "Primary" and "Secondary" Abilities. In Omni there's one collection of approximately 40-50 skills (depending on the exact system used), however most characters have less than ten skills.

Adjustments

The WoD's Arete does not exist in Omni. Instead, use Magic Rating. Characters with special powers (Mages, Vampires, Weres, Wraiths, Changelings, mortals with Numina) will always start with a MR of at least 1. Plain Mortals have a MR of 0. For starting characters going through chargen, calculate it according to the Omni rules (INT + WILL / 2, rounded down). For existing Mage characters being converted, give +1 for each dot of Arete. No character can have a MR higher than 10. When Mages want to buy a new MR point they must go on a Seeking.

Do not use Omni's Psi Rating. Ignore Omni's Piety, Honor, and Renown. WoD Quintessence and Paradox are exactly the same (Use the Wheel from WoD), as are Blood Pool, Gnosis, Pathos, and Glamour. Also brought from the WoD are Humanity, Rage, Corpus, and Banality.

Omni Hit points are a base of 20. Standard humans have 7 health levels in the WoD. For each additional health level above 7, add +5 HP. For those characters with fewer health levels, subtract -5 HP per missing health level. Just as in the WoD, Omni HPs do not change over time. Chargen characters have a one-time chance to increase HPs by +5 or +10. Give all conversion characters a chance at taking a +5 HP or a free Open Talent. Powers that temporarily add or remove health levels would instead add or remove hit points. (+10 improvement may be too much for standard Mortals -- consider restricting +10 to Vampires, Wraiths, Changelings, and Shapechangers.

Do not use the WoD's Nature and Demeanor. We've discarded willpower and so they're not necessary for anything other than RP purposes.

For those really fond of Willpower or running crossover games, you can keep Willpower.

Backgrounds as point-based stats are discarded and represented by the written character background.
