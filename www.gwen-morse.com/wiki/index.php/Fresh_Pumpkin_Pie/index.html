Fresh Pumpkin Pie

I have to admit I don't like the taste of pumpkin pie. However, my son loves it. Since moving to Australia I feel obligated to provide it for him as an American holiday food. While "pumpkin" foods are very popular in Australia it's hard to get a tasty pumpkin pie. Australians tend to prefer roasted chunks of squash, or squash soup. This means I can't just cheat and buy something store bought.

  • 1 1/2 cups pureed squash
  • 1 cup milk or heavy cream
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 3/8 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp mixed spice
  • 1/4 tsp ground clove
  • 2 eggs

Use one butternut squash or two Japanese pumpkins

This makes a 9" pie.

Prep

To roast the squash: cut the squash into quartered wedges. Scoop the seeds out. Place the quartered squash flesh-side down on a rimmed baking sheet lined with foil. Bake until completely tender at 400˚F/200˚C, about 30 to 45 minutes. Larger squash may need an hour.

When soft remove from the skins and puree in the food processor until smooth.

Make a 9" pie crust and use it to line a glass pie plate.

The Pie

Preheat the oven to 375˚F / 190˚C. If using the convection microwave preheat to 200˚C.

Mix all ingredients until smooth. Add the eggs as the last step. Pour the filling into a bottom pie crust that has been blind baked. Before baking, cover the pie rim with aluminum foil to prevent the crimped edges from burning.

Set the pie plate on a cookie sheet to catch any spills. Bake about 65 minutes. In the convection microwave bake on Low Mix at 200˚C for 25 minutes.

Pie Crust

For a single 9" pie crust

  • 1/2 cup salted butter
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 to 4 Tablespoons ice water

Butter should be very cold or frozen. If frozen, grate it using your food processor and let it spin a few times after the flour is added so the flour coats the butter shavings.

Add ice water one tablespoon at a time until the dough just begins to form shaggy clumps. Don't add enough water to create a full ball. The more water you add, the tougher the pie crust.

Press it into a flat disk and refrigerate for 30 minutes before rolling.

Roll out dough on a very lightly floured surface, into a 12” round. The more flour used, the tougher the crust will be. A 12" crust will fit a 9" pie plate. Try to make sure there's a slight dusting of dry flour sticking to the crust when you lift it. This will help keep it from sticking to the pie plate.

When fluting the edge of a pie crust, you should be turning the edge under itself for a double layer of pastry resting on the rim. The thicker edge will cook slower (and release from the pan more easily).

Prick the interior of the pie all over with a fork and then fill the shell with pie weights or dried beans.

Blind bake the pie crust at 425˚F/220˚C for 15 minutes to brown it. In the convection microwave, blind bake on the Convection setting at 220˚C for 15 minutes.
