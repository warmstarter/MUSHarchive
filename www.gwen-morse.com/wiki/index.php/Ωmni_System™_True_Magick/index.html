Ωmni System™ True Magick

Mages in the World of Darkness are able to manipulate the very fabric of reality with only their will (and some props or abilities). This is referred to as "True Magick" (with a "k") to discriminate between it and lesser forms of power. Mage has a highly flexible magic system and I feel it's the reason behind bothering to convert in the first place. See any Mage: The Ascension 2nd Edition or Revised rulebook for specific information on how the system works.

Instead of rolling your arete pool in d10's, you use the d20 Action Table resolution with the following standard difficulties. Instead of using Omni's "spell level" system, we simulate levels with some of the old WoD difficulties.

As a point of reference in the rulebooks, an average difficulty level for spells is 15.

Description Penalty
Coincidental Effect: +3
Vulgar, without Witnesses: +4
Vulgar, with Witnesses: +5
Countermagick (same sphere): +7
Countermagick (different sphere): +8
Counter other Supernaturals: +9
Fast-Casting Penalty: +1
Stacked Effects: +1 per effect after the first
Distant or hidden subject: +1
Monumental Feat: +1 to +3

Paradox is still assigned as normal for Vulgar effects.

Description Penalty
Per sphere level for the highest sphere level used: +1
Per additional sphere for Conjunctional effects: +1
Description Bonus
Per point of quintessence spent ~: -1
Using a Unique Focus: -3 to -5
Near a Node -1 to -3
Extra time spent on Magick: -1 to -3
Prepare researching Lore -1 to -3

~ (decide after the d20 roll, max of 3/turn)

Omni System uses D&D-style hit points. This means that damage is done on a per-HP basis rather than the WoD damage pools/soak/health levels (Note: the myriad combat rolls before finally applying your damage was what pushed me to the Omni system in the first place). Here are some Omni-specific additional difficulties:

Sphere Damage Penalty
Per 2 HP damage (Forces): +1 (a 10hp spell is +5)
Per 1 HP damage (Mind): +2 (a 10 hp spell is +20)
Per 1 HP damage (All others): +1 (a 10hp spell is +10)
