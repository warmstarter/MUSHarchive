New York Style Cheesecake Recipe

I love recipes that can be scaled up or down as needed.

Rule of thumb...for every 8 oz/250g of cream cheese (1 pkg)

  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla

For a whole 8-10" cheesecake, use:

  • the zest of 1 lemon or 1/2 orange
  • 1 Tablespoon corn starch (to help reduce cracking)
  • for extra richness use one small tub of sour cream (around 1/2 cup)

When making your custard, make sure you are creaming the cream cheese and sugar together first. Then add the eggs, one at a time, and vanilla, sour cream, etc. That is a basic recipe that works every time.

A typical 8 inch cheesecake uses 3 packages of cream cheese, 3 eggs, 1 1/2 cups sugar, 1 1/2 tsp vanilla, 1 tablespoon corn starch, 1 tub sour cream.

A typical 10 inch cheesecake uses 4 pkgs of cream cheese: 4 eggs, 2 cups sugar, 2 tsp vanilla, 1 tablespoon corn starch, 1 tub sour cream.

Use the amounts above and you won't go wrong...at least for plain New York style. If you find it too sweet you can reduce it down to 1/3 a cup sugar per package of cream cheese, but any less than that and it affects the taste.

Variations: For a "lemon" cheesecake, for each pack of cream cheese use the zest of one lemon. For the whole cheesecake use the juice of one lemon. For orange cheesecake replace all lemon with half an orange (use zest of one half orange per package and the juice of one half orange per batch of batter). For chocolate cheesecake mix in 8oz (224 grams) semi-sweet chocolate, melted and slightly cooled for one 8-10" cheesecake.

Baking directions: Preheat the oven for at least half an hour to 325 F (160 C). I start it preheating when I'm pulling out my bowls and ingredients. Bake for one hour at 325 F (160 C) in a water bath inside a high-sided roasting pan. There are two ways to do the water bath. With the first, you can wrap the cheesecake pan in a double layer of aluminum foil to keep it watertight, and place the springform pan directly in the pan of water (if you do this add the water after the cheesecake pan is placed inside). The second way is to put a pan of water on the bottom-most oven rack and bake the cheesecake on a separate rack closer to the middle of the oven. This second way acts as a heat sink to hold heat, as well as providing some steam in the oven. Both ways work. Fill the sheet pan with water after you place your cheesecake pan in the oven. After one hour, turn off oven and leave the cheesecake inside until the oven is cool. Then refrigerate cheesecake at least 4-6 hours, or overnight if at all possible. Don't worry if the cheesecake is jiggly all around after one hour baking with the heat on (many directions say it should only be jiggly just in the last 1-2" in the middle -- they're wrong), just make sure its baked long enough to not be "soupy". Jiggly is "set" enough for slow cooling and it means the cheeescake wiggles or jiggles a little when the pan is lightly shaken (like Jello). Soupy means it's still liquid. If it's not cooked enough, bake for another 10 minutes and check it again.

DO NOT PULL IT OUT OF THE OVEN JIGGLY and then immediately refrigerate. It will end up undercooked.

After you confirm the cheesecake isn't soupy, make sure the oven air re-heats before turning it off. This is normally done by waiting for the oven temperature light to turn off.

If you have to take it out of the oven (perhaps to bake something else) have it cool under an inverted metal bowl for a few hours until room temperature, but this is much more likely to crack.

Notes:

  • An 8" cheesecake takes about 1 hour in my oven
  • A 10" cheesecake take about 1 hour and 15 minutes in my oven

To make sour cream topping: Mix two cups sour cream, one teaspoon vanilla and two tablespoons sugar. Spread over top, filling in any cracks, and bake in a 325 F (160 C) oven for 10 minutes if warm, 14-15 minutes if the cheesecake is cool. This is done after the cheesecake is baked.

To make a jam glaze: Stir one-half teaspoon cornstarch into one cup apricot, grape or seedless raspberry jam. Place in medium saucepan and melt on low heat, stirring frequently. Increase heat and bring to a boil. Remove from heat and stir in one tablespoon orange liqueur or rum; strain. Pour over cheesecake. Top with fresh fruit, if desired.

Cheesecake Crust Recipes

Graham Cracker/Cookie cheesecake crust:

  • 1 package Graham crackers or cookies (when ground, about 1 to 1 1/2 cups)
  • 1/4 cup raw or brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup melted butter

Mix all ingredients in a food processor until uniform and slightly sticky. Pat 2/3 of dough on bottom of 9-inch spring-form pan (sides removed). Bake about 8 minutes or till golden; cool. Butter sides of pan; attach to bottom. Pat remaining dough on sides of pan to height of 1 3/4 inches.

Shortbread-style cheesecake crust:

  • 1 cup flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1 slightly beaten egg yolk
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla

Combine first 3 ingredients. Cut in butter till crumbly. Add egg yolk and vanilla; mix well. Pat 1/3 of dough on bottom of 8 or 9-inch spring-form pan (sides removed). Bake about 8 minutes or till golden; cool. Butter sides of pan; attach to bottom. Pat remaining dough on sides of pan to height of 1 3/4 inches.
