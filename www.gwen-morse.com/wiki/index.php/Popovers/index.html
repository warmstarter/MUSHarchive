Popovers (or Yorkshire Pudding)

  • 1 tablespoon melted butter
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup unsifted all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

(extra melted butter for pan)

1. MICROWAVE (100%) butter in a 4-cup glass measure 30 to 40 seconds or until melted. Warm milk in a seperate cup the microwave at the same time. Once both are warm, preheat microwave-convection oven to 450ºF (230ºC). Add a small spoon of melted butter to each cup of the pan. Leave the 6-cup muffin or cupcake pan in the microwave while it pre-heats to heat the pan as well.

2. Add milk and butter to the blender (I use my Magic Bullet with the blender jug). Add eggs; blend for about 30 seconds. Add flour and salt, blend again just until smooth (do not overbeat or popovers will not pop). Set batter aside to rest until the oven is pre-heated. Pour batter into each cup, filling it about 1/3 to 1/2 of the way, and place on the low/bottom rack.

3. Convection Microwave: Convection-only bake on low rack at 400 F/200C for 30 to 35 minutes or until puffed and golden brown. Pierce when done to let steam escape. Serve piping hot.

Makes 6 muffin size Popovers (or 9-12 cupcake size)

Notes:

  • These popovers were mastered as part of trying to make them in my Convection Microwave. It turned out I just needed to preheat the oven to a higher temperature than I would use for baking, and use the Convection setting (not the mixed-mode bake setting). The recipe is standard. Add an extra egg and let them collapse in the middle (to look like little cups) and you have individual Yorkshire puddings.
  • A larger-sized Yorkshire Pudding can also be cooked in a cake pan. Use the same mixture as above, except using 3 eggs. Heat greased or buttered 8"x8" square cake pan, or 9" round cake pan until the oven is heated to preheat the oven to 220 C (450 F), add batter mixture and bake about 15 minutes. Without opening the oven door, turn oven temperature down to 190 C (375 F) and bake for another 10 - 15 min. The above recipe is enough for one cake pan.
  • For absolutely "traditional" Yorkshire pudding replace the butter with 1 tablespoon pan drippings from a roast.

Variations:

Standard popovers are delicious, but there are many variations that make them that much better. Anything you enjoy adding to pancakes can also be added to popover mix (try 1/4 cup of fresh blueberries).

  • Cinnamon Bun: 1 batch of Popover batter with the addition of 1 tsp vanilla extract. Make a paste using 4 Tbsp butter (about 50 grams), 3 Tbsp brown sugar, 2 tsp ground cinnamon, 1 tsp ground ginger. Put paste in a piping bag or roll wax paper into a piping bag shape. Add batter in each cup of the pan. Squeeze into each a swirl of butter paste. Bake as above. Top with white glaze (2 cups icing sugar with a few tsp of water or orange juice). Be careful; adding too much paste will keep the popovers from rising.
  • Chocolate: 3 Tbsp. unsweetened cocoa powder. 4 Tbsp. sugar, mixed into the batter.
  • Dessert Popovers: 1 batch of Popover batter with the addition of 1 Tbsp sugar and 1 tsp vanilla extract. Bake as above. While still hot dip the lids into melted chocolate. Cut open and fill with a scoop of frozen ice cream. Serve immediately.
  • Apple Crumble: 1 batch of Popover batter with the addition of 1 finely chopped apple and 1 tsp vanilla extract, mixed into the batter. Top with a sprinkle of crumble mix: 1/4 cup rolled oats, 1 Tablespoon brown sugar, 1 Tablespooon very soft butter, 1/4 tsp each: cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves (or 1/2 tsp mixed pie spice), all mashed together. Bake as above.
  • BLT Steakhouse Gruyere Popovers: 1 batch of Popover batter. Add batter to each cup of the pan. Top each with about 2 Tbsp grated Gruyere cheese. Bake as above. Note: It should take about 1 cup of grated cheese for a batch this size.
  • Parmesan: grate up a small chunk of fresh parmesan (about 1" cube) and blend into 1 batch of Popover batter. Bake as above.
  • Cheddar Cheese and Chives: 1 batch of Popover batter with the addition of 1 Tbsp fresh minced chives, and 1 cup grated sharp cheddar.
  • Cheddar and Cayenne Pepper: 1/4 tsp. cayenats, 1 Tbsp sugar, 1/4 tsp each: cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves (or 1/2 tsp mixed pie spice). Bake as above.ne pepper. 1/3 cup sharp cheddar, shredded, mixed into the batter.
  • Bacon and Asiago: 3 strips of bacon, cooked and crumbled. 1/3 cup Asiago, shredded, mixed into the batter.
